Gaming on Linux

Have you ever thought about gaming on Linux? If you use Steam and want to try Linux, then worry no longer; due to Valve's effort, gaming has never been easier. The first step is always the hardest: choosing a Linux distro to stick with. If you don't want any headaches and want something that works out of the box, look no further than Linux Mint and Bazzite.

Linux Mint is a Debian/Ubuntu-based distro known for how simple it is for beginners. All it takes to get set up is to install Steam from Mint's own app store. The rest is just as straightforward, with the only added step of choosing a version of Proton, which allows you to play most PC games on Linux.

While Mint is a good choice, Bazzite is more suited for those looking for better performance. Bazzite takes after Valve's own SteamOS, allowing for a normal desktop or a console-like experience. It comes pre-installed with the tools you need, including Steam and other launchers. Thanks to Steam and the Linux community, the days of being stuck on Windows for gaming are gone.
